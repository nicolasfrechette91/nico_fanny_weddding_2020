Jose Guadalupe Posada Museum

Posada was an artist and caricaturist whose art was often combined with socio-political criticism. He is probably best known for his calacas and calaveras, the skeletons whose dark humor delighted the public. In his museum, you can see its more than three thousand exhibits

The museum is next to the Encino Church. You can attend engraving classes or enjoy a cultural event on Sunday at midday.

Lado norte Jardín del Encino s/n, Barrio Del Encino, 20240

Museo Nacional de la Muerte

The museum shown hundreds of death-related artifacts. It also includes the direction of depicting the historic role of death’s iconography and the funereal arts within Mexican culture, both contemporary and ancient. The Museum’s curators conveyed the wide range of Mexican society’s relationship towards death and Santa Muerte himself, ranging from the popular portrayal of a people who are always joking and laughing about mortality, to the nitty gritty — sometimes quite graphic — reality of death most corporeal from the pre-hispanic era to pop culture.

Rivero y Gutiérrez x, José María Morelos y Pavón, Zona Centro, 20000

Ferrocarrilero Aguascalientes

The museum is housed in the old loading depot built at the end of the nineteenth century and used to weigh and distribute a wide range of goods transported on the railway.

Today the museum has a collection of more than ten thousand pieces related to railway infrastructure, including machinery, tools, equipment and documents. The goal is to tell the story and display the history of the national railway system and its economic, political, social and cultural impact on the development of Aguascalientes in the early twentieth century.

Plaza de las Tres Centurias, 28 de Agosto S/N, Barrio de la Estación, 20259

Museo del Juguete Tradicional

The collection displays some amazingly innovative traditional toys, made from a range of unusual materials. The exhibition consists of pieces from all over Mexico.
